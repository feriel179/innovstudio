Villa Lumière

A glass-walled family home built around a sculptural staircase and a private courtyard flooded with southern light.

TypePrivate Residence
LocationSaint-Germain, Paris
Year2025
Surface380 m²
StatusCompleted

Light as the primary material.

Villa Lumière was designed around a single obsession: maximising the quality of light at every hour of the day. The south-facing plot in Saint-Germain allowed us to orient the main living spaces along a glazed spine that tracks the arc of the sun from morning kitchen to evening salon.

The structural heart of the home is a floating concrete staircase that rises three floors through a double-height void. It doubles as a light shaft, drawing daylight from a rooflight above down into the lower levels. Around it, rooms open and close through pivoting panels rather than conventional doors.

Materials were kept deliberately quiet — raw linen plaster, honed limestone floors, and black steel frames — so that the changing light becomes the decoration. The private courtyard garden brings greenery and birdsong to the heart of the plan.
